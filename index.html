Chadwick Boseman

Chadwick Boseman was an American actor known for his portrayals of real-life historical figures such as Jackie Robinson in 42, James Brown in Get on Up, and Thurgood Marshall in Marshall. He was also known for his portrayal of the superhero Black Panther in the Marvel Cinematic Universe films, including Captain America: Civil War, Black Panther, Avengers: Infinity War, and Avengers: Endgame.

Boseman was born and raised in Anderson, South Carolina, and attended college at Howard University in Washington, D.C. He began his career as a writer and director, and in 2003 he landed his first television role in an episode of Third Watch. He went on to appear in a number of television shows and films, including Law & Order, ER, and The Express: The Ernie Davis Story.

Boseman's breakout role came in 2013 when he was cast as Jackie Robinson in the biographical film 42. He received critical acclaim for his performance, and the film was a commercial success. Boseman went on to star in a number of other biographical films, including Get on Up, Marshall, and Da 5 Bloods.

Black Panther was a critical and commercial success, and Boseman's performance was widely praised. He went on to appear in Avengers: Infinity War and Avengers: Endgame, which were also critical and commercial successes.

Boseman died on August 28, 2020, at the age of 43, after a four-year battle with colon cancer. His death was met with an outpouring of grief and tributes from fans, fellow actors, and public figures.
